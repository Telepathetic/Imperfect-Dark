# POTENTIOMETER

### Signal Control for Imperfect Dark

-----

Not all arenas run at full power.

Imperfect Dark is a system, not a fixed setting.  
The arena, the rules, and the experience can be tuned.

This document describes how to adjust the signal.

Turn the dial to match the pilots.  
Then turn it down.

-----

## THE DIAL

A potentiometer does not switch between states.  
It moves along a continuous range.

So does Imperfect Dark.

At full signal: visibility, clarity, control.  
As the signal falls: uncertainty increases.

Turn the dial down until sight is no longer enough.

-----

## HIGH SIGNAL

The arena is fully visible.

Robots are understood by their pilots at all times.  
Control comes first.

**Suggested settings:**

- Arena lights on or bots equipped with bright LEDs  
- No weapons, or soft contact only  
- Objective-based play:
  - rescue
  - transport
  - mapping

**Purpose:**

- Learn movement  
- Learn control  
- Learn how designs behave in real terrain  

This is where pilots begin.

-----

## MID SIGNAL

The arena begins to withhold.

Pilots no longer have complete information.  
Robots begin to act as extensions, not tools.

**Suggested settings:**

- Reduced lighting or obstructed visibility  
- Limited interaction between robots  
- Mixed objectives and interference  

**Purpose:**

- Introduce uncertainty  
- Develop sensing strategies  
- Encourage adaptation under constraint  

The system starts to push back.

-----

## LOW SIGNAL

The signal fades.

The arena is no longer directly visible.  
What happens inside must be inferred.

- No information beyond onboard sensing  
- Terrain defines the engagement  

This is Imperfect Dark.

-----

## ADJUSTMENT

There is no fixed progression.

Different pilots, different arenas, different paths.

Dials are for fiddling with. 

Turn the dial down when:
- pilots show confidence  
- outcomes are understood  
- curiosity exceeds hesitation  

Turn the dial up when:
- frustration replaces learning  
- fear replaces curiosity  
- systems become opaque  

-----

## LOCAL CONTROL

Each arena sets its own dial.

There is no central authority.  
No global standard.

What matters is continuity:
- pilots understand the current setting  
- changes are intentional  
- the system remains legible  

Record settings if useful.  
Or don’t. ¯\_(ツ)_/¯
